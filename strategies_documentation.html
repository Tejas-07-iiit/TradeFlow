Strategy Execution Lifecycle

A generalized overview of what happens when strategies pass or fail the TradeFlow engine

Strategy-To-Execution Pipeline

➔
🤖 2. Strategy Suite Evaluate Rules
➔
⚖️ 3. Scorer & Fusion Weighted Consensus
➔
🧠 4. AI / Risk Filter Veto & Sizing
➔
💸 5. Order Fill Paper Position

✓ When a Strategy "Passes" (Triggers)

A strategy "passes" (triggers an active signal) when the real-time technical indicators align with its core mathematical logic rules (e.g. RSI is oversold and the asset touches a lower Bollinger Band during low ADX).

1. Signal Generation The strategy returns a status of BUY (Long) or SELL (Short) alongside a confidence score (e.g. 85%) and detailed textual reasoning.
2. Consensus Vote Ingestion The active signal is added to the Fusion Engine. The engine tabulates votes across all 29 strategies to determine if there is a majority consensus.
3. AI Coordinator Prefilter Pass If the consensus score exceeds the threshold, the LLM Coordinator or rule engine builds a trade proposal and assesses its setup quality.
4. Risk Checks & Execution If it clears risk parameters (volatility, leverage, and news sentiment checks), the trade gets sized, and a PENDING order is fired. It is matched and executed as an open position.

✗ When a Strategy "Fails" (Neutral / Veto)

A strategy "fails" (or is ignored) when the market structure does not satisfy its rules. Alternatively, a strategy's triggered signal can "fail" pre-trade risk validation gates.

1. Natural Rejection (HOLD) If indicators are neutral, the strategy returns a HOLD signal (default state) with low confidence (e.g. 25%). The vote is marked neutral and discarded.
2. Macro Trend Veto A strategy may generate a BUY signal, but if the price is trading below the 200 EMA macro filter, the structural trend filter vetoes it. It is classified as REJECTED.
3. News Sentiment Veto If a strategy is bullish but the LLM News Classifier detects negative news matching high-risk keywords (e.g. bankrupt, hack, SEC investigation), it overrides the strategy and cancels the trade.
4. Position / Risk Veto If the asset already has an open position or a pending order, or if your wallet balance is too low, the order is blocked to prevent double exposure and margin calls.
