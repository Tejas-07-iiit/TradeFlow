1. System Overview

▼

TradeFlow is a highly sophisticated, institutional-grade automated paper trading system that integrates multi-strategy technical analysis, Large Language Model (LLM) orchestration, and robust dynamic risk management.

Core Modules

  • Trading Engine src/server/background-runner.ts: Orchestrator polling Binance WS.
  • Strategy Engine src/strategy-core/*: Multi-factor pipeline grouped into Orthogonal Families.
  • Fusion Engine src/strategy-core/fusion/index.ts: Computes Herfindahl-Hirschman derived effectiveN.
  • AI Orchestrator src/services/ai/orchestrator/pipeline.ts: Groq-powered cognitive arbiter.
  • Trade Manager src/services/trade-manager/*: Dynamic R-multiple TP/SL adjustments.

2. Full Trade Lifecycle

▼

The exact execution flow traces as follows:

  1. Ingestion: Binance WS streams 5m klines to cache.
  2. Evaluation: runStrategyPipeline computes shared indicators and evaluates 30+ strategies.
  3. Fusion: fuseStrategies clusters strategies by family to calculate familyNetDirection and effectiveN.
  4. AI Validation: getStrategyDecision passes the snapshot to the LLM for setup quality and confidence scoring. Rate-limits trigger localFallbackDecision.
  5. Risk Gating: computeRiskAdjustedSize applies vol-targeting and peak drawdown scaling.
  6. Execution: createPaperOrderInternal creates pending orders; matched instantly via Prisma transaction, reserving marginUsed.
  7. Management: runAiTradeManagerLoop triggers early AI exits or adjusts SL/TP using R-multiples and health scores.
  8. Exit: Position closes, atomically returning Realized PnL to the wallet balance.

3. Strategy Engine

▼

The strategy engine evaluates independent market anomalies using the crucial concept of Orthogonality Clusters (Families).

Family-Aware Logic

Legacy systems suffer from false consensus by double-counting correlated strategies (e.g., 10 MA crossovers firing simultaneously). TradeFlow clusters strategies by latent factor (trend, reversion, volatility, etc.).

Formulas:

// Family-level contribution = mean across members (1/N within-family)
meanSigned = bucket.signedWeight / bucket.members;

// Effective N: (Σw)² / Σw² on absolute contributions
effectiveN = (sumW * sumW) / sumW2;

If effectiveN approaches 1, the LLM is warned that despite high alignment, only one latent factor is driving the signal.

4. Confidence System

▼

Confidence is a multi-layered aggregate:

  • Strategy Level: Base confidence generated by deterministic rules.
  • Regime Weight: Multiplier (0-1) adjusting confidence based on MarketRegime fit.
  • Family Alignment: Percentage of absolute weight agreeing with the net direction.
  • LLM Validation: Final confidence generated by the AI reasoning engine.
  • Dynamic Confidence: Active trades experience confidence decay via calculateDynamicConfidence (factoring consecutive adverse candles and momentum breakdown).

5. Take Profit / Stop Loss Engine

▼

TradeFlow abandons static targets for a living risk perimeter (dynamic-levels.ts).

  • Initial: SL = ATR * 2, TP = SL * 1.8
  • Break-even: Triggers when PnL reaches threshold OR if confidence decays to 50-59%. SL moves to entry ± buffer.
  • Profit Protection:
    - +1R locks in 0.25R.
    - +2R locks in 1.0R.
    - +3R locks in 2.0R.
  • Trailing Stops:
    - Volatility Regime: 4.0x ATR
    - Trend Regime: 3.5x ATR
    - Choppy Regime: 2.0x ATR
    Tightens automatically for late-stage trends (>25 candles).

Safety Guards: Adjustments are clamped to 15% of the remaining distance to price to prevent noise stop-outs. SL modifications are strictly monotonic (can only tighten).

6. Dynamic Trade Management

▼

The evaluatePosition background loop runs every 30s.

Exit Intelligence

Aggregates technical anomalies into an exit confidence score:

  • EMA Crosses (0.25 weight)
  • Regime Shifts (0.20 weight)
  • MACD Collapse (0.15 weight)
  • Candlestick Pattern Reversals (0.15 weight)
  • News Shock (+25% absolute boost)

Exits trigger at confidence >= 70 but require Candle Confirmation Gate (e.g., 3 consecutive adverse candles, scaled by volatility) unless overridden by a Liquidation Move.

7. Risk Management System

▼

computeRiskAdjustedSize standardizes risk before execution.

// 1. Volatility Target Multiplier
volTargetMultiplier = 0.015 / (atrPct / 100);

// 2. Drawdown Gate
drawdownPct = (peakEquity - currentEquity) / peakEquity;
drawdownMultiplier = Math.pow(1 - (drawdownPct / maxDrawdown), exponent);

// 3. Final Size
riskAmount = totalEquity * baseRiskPct * volTargetMultiplier * drawdownMultiplier;

Additionally, hard exposure limits veto trades if a single symbol exceeds the maximum open positions notional cap.

8. AI / LLM Orchestration

▼

The LLM operates as the strategic arbiter via executeDecisionJob.

  • Routing: Dynamically selects llama-3.3-70b-versatile for execution-critical jobs.
  • Fail-Fast Degradation: Instantly downgrades to qwen3-32b on 429 limits or un-availability.
  • Local Fallback Engine: If all providers fail, localFallbackDecision takes over. It caps sizing to 25% and enforces strict entry thresholds (alignmentScore >= 70).

9. Regime Detection

▼

Classifies market state using ADX, ATR, and MA slopes.

States: Trending Up, Trending Down, Choppy, High Volatility.

Directly alters execution by zeroing out regimeWeight for trend strategies in choppy markets, and dynamically scaling the trailing stop ATR multipliers.

10. Telemetry & Analytics

▼

saveExplainableSignal records the exact technical variables, LLM JSON reasoning, strategy snapshots, and candlestick anomalies for every accepted or rejected decision.

TradeManagementEvent logs every dynamic SL/TP modification with the health score context.

11. Database & Persistence

▼

Prisma models mimic Binance isolated margin accounts.

PaperWallet tracks balance and usedMargin. PaperPosition tracks entry, exits, and PnL. The system prevents double-closing via CAS (Compare-and-Swap) updates.

12. Frontend / UI Logic

▼

Server actions push live PnL and trade events to the client. The frontend charts dynamically render the audit trail of `TradeManagementEvent` logs, visualising trailing stops and health adjustments over time.

13. Current Weaknesses

▼
  • Polling Over-reliance: Dependent on the 1s and 30s loops; intra-tick volatility relies on backfill.
  • Backfill Slippage Gap: Startup backfills assume execution at exact SL prices, ignoring realistic market slippage during downtime.
  • Over-parameterization: dynamic-levels.ts has dense rule branching that may conflict during extreme volatility bursts.

14. Current Strengths

▼
  • Institutional Fusion: effectiveN family-aware decorrelation prevents false consensus.
  • Safety Abstractions: The local fallback engine and strict Prisma CAS ensure robust, non-blocking execution.
  • Living Risk Management: Transitioning from static TP/SL to R-multiple and health-based tracking mimics real desk execution.

15. Final System Assessment

▼

TradeFlow is a mature, production-ready quantitative platform. Its blend of deterministic technical engines with qualitative AI arbitration represents institutional-grade architecture. The statistical realism of its risk scaling and the robustness of its execution orchestrator make it highly resilient.
